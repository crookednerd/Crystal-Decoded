🟡 Time Pressure & Interruptions

Red-mode stress: You can flip to being unusually demanding, rigid, and impatient — a side that surprises people who only know your gentle nature.

Growth Edge

How You Handle Feedback

Your relationship with feedback and criticism is nuanced — and understanding it is one of your biggest growth opportunities.

🛡️

Your Default Response

You have an ever-present internal critic that already judges everything you think and do. So when external criticism arrives, it lands on someone who's already being hard on themselves. You tend to take feedback personally rather than objectively, and may need processing time before you can hear the useful parts.

💡

What Actually Helps

Feedback delivered calmly, privately, and with genuine care lands much better. Frame it around growth rather than failure. Avoid public comparisons to other people — that's a fast track to shutting down. Give you time to reflect before expecting a response.

🎯

The Opportunity

Your profile suggests that reducing sensitivity to constructive criticism could unlock a lot of growth. The helpful information often sits inside feedback that initially feels uncomfortable. Your internal critic is often right — building confidence in your own judgment would let you trust it more.

⚖️

Decision Making & Criticism

You prefer gut-feel decisions over exhaustive analysis, and you're often correct. But a lack of confidence in your own judgment means you may second-guess yourself. You may not express yourself forcefully enough, leading to being misunderstood — which then feels like rejection.

Red drops 34%: This is the biggest insight. You actively dial UP your decisive, action-oriented energy at work — but it's not natural at rest. Your instinctive self has very low Red (18%), meaning the assertiveness takes conscious effort.

The Big Takeaway

  • Impose excessive structure and formality

How This Shows Up at Work

Your colour profile shapes everything from how you handle meetings to how you deal with that one coworker who won't stop emailing in ALL CAPS.

🏢

Your Work Persona Is Intentional

Your conscious profile shows you're actively dialing up both Green (empathy) and Red (action) at work — 34% more than your natural resting state for each. That means you're spending real energy to be both the person who cares about the team AND the person who pushes things forward. That effort is admirable, but it has a cost. If you've ever come home feeling drained even after a "good" day, this is probably why.

🗣️

Meetings & Collaboration

You're the person who makes sure the quiet voice in the room gets heard. You naturally seek consensus and democratic decisions, which makes you invaluable in collaborative settings.

The catch: you may check that everyone is comfortable before checking that the decision is actually made. You can also get distracted from listening attentively — people should gently confirm you're tracking if a meeting runs long.

🔥

Conflict at Work

You avoid it. Hard. You'd rather absorb discomfort than create it. If someone makes a mistake, you're more likely to sympathize with them and spread the blame around than to address it directly.

This peacekeeper instinct serves you well 80% of the time. But the other 20% — when accountability genuinely matters — your avoidance can let small issues become big ones. The profile's blunt advice: sometimes you need to say the hard thing, even when it feels terrible.

📋

Deadlines & Structure

You don't naturally adhere to rigid timelines. You need processing time that others may not understand, and demanding an instant response from you will usually backfire.

This doesn't mean you're unreliable — you're described as conscientious, industrious, and dependable. It means your reliability runs on its own internal clock. Having a manager or system that helps you monitor agreed deadlines (rather than imposing them) works best.

🧭

Monitoring Others' Work

If your role requires you to oversee detailed work from other people, be aware: your profile flags this as something you'll find uninteresting, stressful, and exhausting. It drains your Green energy without feeding it. If you can, delegate the monitoring and focus on the mentoring instead — that's where your strengths actually live.

💡

Your Creative Edge at Work

You're an ingenious, original problem solver. But your creative ideas can sometimes land ahead of where the group is, leading to misunderstandings. Try presenting ideas as options rather than conclusions — your profile says you actually prefer receiving information that way too, so extend the same approach outward.

This is the "hidden fist" your profile describes. It doesn't come out often, but when it does, it's real. Giving the people close to you a heads-up about this side of you (and what triggers it) can prevent a lot of confusion.
